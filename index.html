Projects

Facial Recogniton Door Lock

Tired of having to open the doors while your hands are full? I built a facial recognition door lock using the Raspberry Pi in Python using OpenCV and pre-trained classifier using Haar cascades. The program would prompt users for a name, take twenty pictures, use the classifier to effectively detect faces, and then train a recognizer algorithm. The final recognizer would output a confidence score with >95% accuracy after testing on a larger dataset! The entire product also fit into my hand and could fit easily onto any door. I presented this product in my Entreprenuership Creativity Class (PSYSCH 223) where it was voted the #1 project by students/staff and then to the CPGO VC Fund where it was a finalist. The product is currenlty in the works to being used at the University of Michigan Dearborn to improve their faculty accesses.

Politisense

Built during PennApps XXI, I thought it'd be interesting to present our take on an alternative to political polls. Thus, the product "PolitiSense" was born. PolitiSense scrapes data on hundreds of thousands of headlines, posts and comments across the internet, gathering the opinions of the masses from tweets on Twitter or subreddits on Reddit while also sifting through volumes of news headlines from major media outlets such as Fox or CNN. It then analyzes what people are saying about a particular political figure at any given time, calculating sentiment with word content and level of engagement (number of likes, retweets, upvotes, etc) and aggregating into a single sentiment score. Feel free to visit the link above to see it in action!

Wikipedia Graph Network

The Wikipedia Game is a game that basically pits random players against one another to find the fastest link to a specified Wikipedia article from an arbitrary starting point. I built a Chrome extension capable of finding the shortest route between these chosen articles and beat any human player ;). I built a chrome extension that used a set of API calls to a database with all of the English Wikipedia Links. This database effectively stored Wikipedia as a Graph Network using Stanford's SNAP project and then utilized A-star's algorithm to find the shortest route. The extension ended up being first place in all games for about two weeks before getting turned of (so sorry if you ran into a mr.totallynotabot and instantly lost at same point)
